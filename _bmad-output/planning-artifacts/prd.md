# Product Requirements Document - ch3ss

**Author:** Anthony
**Date:** 2026-03-08

## Executive Summary

Ch3ss is a mobile-first Progressive Web App that reimagines chess as a casual, low-friction experience. At each turn, the player picks one of 3 moves generated by Stockfish at varying quality levels — Top (best move), Correct (solid move), Bof (suboptimal move) — without knowing which is which. The result: a real chess game where tactical depth is preserved but cognitive load drops to near-zero. Designed for micro-sessions (2-3 minutes per game), ch3ss targets chess players who love the game but want a relaxed, instinctive experience — in the metro, on a break, between meetings.

The AI opponent plays freely at a configurable ELO (800-1600). The chess engine runs entirely client-side (Stockfish.js), enabling offline play with zero server infrastructure. No account required at MVP — open the app, play immediately, replay instantly.

**Project Classification:** Web App (PWA, mobile-first, offline-capable) · Entertainment / Casual Gaming · Low complexity · Greenfield

### What Makes This Special

No existing chess product reduces the decision space while preserving the feel of a real game. Puzzles are isolated exercises. Variants like Chess960 add complexity. Classical online chess demands full concentration. Ch3ss occupies an empty niche: a complete chess game that feels effortless.

The core insight is that chess paralysis comes from the open field of possibilities, not from the rules. Constrain the options to 3 credible moves, and the game becomes instinctively playable. Players still build their win or loss through their choices — but without the anxiety of "what am I missing?" The blind selection mechanic (no quality labels visible) adds a layer of intuitive pattern recognition that becomes its own reward.

## Success Criteria

### User Success

- **"Aha" moment:** The player finishes a game and realizes they never agonized over a move, yet the game was tactically interesting. They immediately want to play again.
- **Session depth:** Players naturally chain ≥3 games per session — the replay friction is low enough that "one more" is the default.
- **Zero learning curve:** A chess player picks up the 3-move mechanic instantly on first game, no tutorial needed.

### Business Success

- **Onboarding funnel:** High conversion at every step — first launch → first game completed → second game started. Drop-off at any step signals a UX problem.
- **Retention J7:** Meaningful return rate within 7 days (target to be defined post-launch with real data).
- **Net growth:** More players joining/staying than leaving, sustained over the first months. No volume target — the signal is the trajectory, not the number.
- **3-month signal:** To be defined post-launch based on observed usage patterns.

### Technical Success

Performance targets are defined in Non-Functional Requirements (NFR1-NFR6). Key thresholds: <200ms move generation, <400ms UI interactions, <3s initial load, full offline capability.

## Product Scope

### MVP - Minimum Viable Product

1. Playable chessboard — 100% standard rules, mobile-first UI
2. 3-move selection mechanic — Stockfish analyzes position, presents Top/Correct/Bof moves with no quality labels
3. AI opponent — plays freely at configurable ELO (800-1600), ~1s artificial delay for natural rhythm
4. Local engine — Stockfish.js client-side, zero server dependency
5. End conditions — checkmate, stalemate, voluntary resignation
6. Local save — current game persisted via localStorage, restored on reopen
7. Instant replay — new game starts immediately after completion, zero friction
8. Settings — ELO selector, game history, light/dark theme toggle

### Growth Features (Post-MVP)

- Player ELO that evolves across games (wins/losses in ch3ss)
- "Invite a friend" sharing mechanic
- Game history browser
- Lightweight monetization (donations, cosmetic themes/piece sets)

### Vision (Future)

- Multiplayer — games against other humans, both players constrained by the 3-move mechanic
- Store publication — iOS App Store + Google Play Store

## User Journeys

### Journey 1: First Discovery — "Le joueur de métro"

**Karim, 28, ELO ~1100 on Lichess.** Plays chess a few times a week with friends, follows GM streams, but avoids rated games on his phone — he makes dumb moves when distracted and hates watching his ELO drop. Right now he's on the metro, 15 minutes to kill.

**Opening Scene:** Karim sees ch3ss mentioned in a Reddit thread about casual chess. "Choose from 3 moves instead of playing freely" — he's intrigued. He taps the link on his phone. No signup, no account creation. The app loads instantly.

**Rising Action:** A chessboard appears. It's his turn — three squares are highlighted on the board. He doesn't know which move is best, he just picks one that feels right. The AI responds in about a second. Three new options appear. He picks again. The rhythm is fast, instinctive. He's not calculating 4 moves ahead — he's reading the board and trusting his gut.

**Climax:** 12 moves in, he spots a tactic in one of the 3 options — a knight fork. He picks it, wins material, and the game snowballs. He wins by checkmate. Total time: 2 minutes. He didn't agonize once.

**Resolution:** "New Game" is right there. He taps it. Three games later, his metro stop arrives. He closes the app mid-game, knowing it'll be there when he comes back. He's already thinking about playing again on his lunch break.

### Journey 2: The Returning Player — Interrupted Session

**Karim again, next morning.** He opens ch3ss on the bus. The game he left mid-move yesterday is right there — board state restored, his turn. He picks a move and continues. The game ends in a draw (stalemate). He taps "New Game," adjusts the AI ELO from 1000 to 1200 in settings because yesterday felt too easy, and starts a tougher game. He loses this one — the AI played sharper moves. He shrugs, starts another. No ELO anxiety, no ranked ladder stress. Just games.

### Journey 3: Edge Cases & Recovery

**Scenario A — Offline play:** Karim enters the subway tunnel. Network drops. He's mid-game. Nothing changes — Stockfish runs locally, the board responds instantly. He finishes the game underground, starts another. He never noticed the network was gone.

**Scenario B — Confusing board state:** Early in his ch3ss experience, Karim sees 3 move options but none seem good — his position is losing. He picks one reluctantly. The game continues. He realizes the mechanic works both ways: when you're losing, even the "best" option may not save you. That's chess. He doesn't blame the app.

**Scenario C — App closed accidentally:** Karim's phone rings mid-game. He takes the call, the browser gets killed by the OS. When he reopens ch3ss later, the game is exactly where he left it (localStorage). No progress lost.

## Innovation & Novel Patterns

### Detected Innovation Areas

**1. Decision-Space Reduction for Chess**
No existing chess product constrains a full game to 3 move choices per turn. Puzzles isolate positions, variants change rules, but ch3ss is the first to apply decision-space reduction to a complete, standard chess game. The player plays real chess — just with fewer options per turn.

**2. Blind Quality Selection**
The 3 moves are presented without quality labels. The player doesn't know which is Top, Correct, or Bof. This creates an intuitive pattern-recognition layer: over time, players develop a feel for which move "looks right" — a form of passive chess learning without pedagogical pressure.

**3. Eval-Loss Bucket Algorithm**
Stockfish multi-PV analysis classifies moves into quality tiers using centipawn thresholds: Top (eval-loss 0 to T1), Correct (T1 to T2), Bof (>T2). Default thresholds: T1=30cp, T2=100cp, depth=12. This produces 3 credible moves that span a realistic quality range — never a caricaturally bad option.

### Validation Approach

- **Method:** Informal playtesting with friends and close testers
- **Focus:** Does the 3-move mechanic feel fun? Is the blind selection engaging or frustrating? Do players want to replay?
- **No formal KPIs** — gut-feel validation at this stage. The product either clicks or it doesn't.

### Risk Mitigation

- **If blind selection doesn't feel fun:** The project gets killed. No pivot to showing quality labels — the blind mechanic IS the product. If it doesn't work, the concept doesn't work.
- **If thresholds feel wrong:** T1/T2 thresholds and Stockfish depth are adjustable via dev settings. Tuning is expected during playtesting.

## Web App Specific Requirements

### Project-Type Overview

Ch3ss is a **Single Page Application (SPA)** — one screen, no routing, no page transitions. The entire experience lives on a single view: the chessboard with move selection, settings drawer, and game-over overlay. PWA-enabled for Add to Home Screen on mobile.

### Browser Support

- **Target:** Modern evergreen browsers only — Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Edge (latest 2 versions)
- **No legacy support** — no IE11, no older mobile browsers
- **Key dependency:** WebAssembly support required for Stockfish.js (supported by all target browsers)

### SEO & Social Sharing

- **SEO:** Minimal — no need for SSR or pre-rendering. The app is a game, not content.
- **Social metadata:** Open Graph and Twitter Card meta tags for link sharing (title, description, preview image). When someone shares the ch3ss URL on social media or messaging apps, it should render a compelling card.

### Real-Time Performance

- **Stockfish engine:** Runs in a Web Worker to keep the UI thread free. Move analysis happens in real-time without blocking interactions.
- **Board responsiveness:** Touch/click interactions respond immediately (<16ms frame budget). Move animations are smooth (60fps).
- **AI response rhythm:** ~1s artificial delay after Stockfish computes, to create natural game pacing (not instant, not slow).

### Responsive Design

- **Mobile-first:** Primary design target is mobile portrait (360-428px width)
- **Tablet/desktop:** Scales gracefully but not the primary design focus
- **Touch-optimized:** Large tap targets for move selection on mobile, no hover-dependent interactions

### Accessibility

Basic accessibility level. Measurable requirements defined in NFR7-NFR10. Key aspects: keyboard navigation, ARIA labels for screen readers, WCAG AA color contrast, reduced-motion support. Not targeting full WCAG AA compliance at MVP.

### PWA Requirements

- **Service Worker:** Cache app shell and Stockfish WASM binary for offline play
- **Web App Manifest:** App name, icons, theme color, standalone display mode
- **Installable:** Meets Chrome/Safari criteria for Add to Home Screen prompt
- **Offline:** Full functionality after initial load — no network dependency for gameplay

### Implementation Considerations

- **Bundle size:** Stockfish WASM binary is the largest asset (~2-4MB). Must be loaded asynchronously and cached aggressively via Service Worker.
- **Web Worker isolation:** Stockfish runs in a dedicated Web Worker. Communication via `postMessage` for move requests/responses.
- **localStorage limits:** ~5MB per origin. Game state is small (FEN string + move history), well within limits. Game history list may need pruning strategy if many games are saved.
- **No backend:** Zero server-side code at MVP. Static hosting (Vercel, Netlify, GitHub Pages) is sufficient.

## Project Scoping & Phased Development

### MVP Strategy & Philosophy

**MVP Approach:** Experience MVP — the sole purpose is to validate that the 3-move blind mechanic feels fun. Everything else (accounts, ELO tracking, sharing) is noise until this is proven.

**Resource:** Solo developer. No backend, no infrastructure overhead, no features requiring ongoing operational work. All 3 user journeys fully supported at MVP. Nothing in the MVP feature set (see Product Scope) can be cut — every item directly supports the core experience validation.

### Risk Mitigation Strategy

**Technical Risk — Stockfish.js performance (HIGH):**
The 200ms move generation target on mid-range mobile is unvalidated. Stockfish WASM in a Web Worker may be slower than expected on low-end devices.
- *Mitigation:* Prototype Stockfish.js performance early — this is the first dev task before any UI work. If <200ms is unreachable, evaluate: reducing analysis depth, using a lighter engine (lc0, custom), or adjusting the target.
- *Go/no-go gate:* If move generation consistently exceeds 500ms on mid-range mobile, reassess feasibility.

**Market Risk — mechanic validation (HIGH):**
The blind 3-move mechanic might not feel fun. No comparable product exists to reference.
- *Mitigation:* Ship a playable prototype fast, playtest with friends. Binary outcome: fun or kill the project.

**Resource Risk — solo dev burnout (MEDIUM):**
One developer doing UI, engine integration, PWA, and playtesting.
- *Mitigation:* Lean stack, zero backend, leverage existing libraries (chess.js for rules, chessboard library for UI, Stockfish.js for engine). Don't build what you can import.

## Functional Requirements

### Game Play

- FR1: Player can view a chessboard with standard piece placement and positioning
- FR2: Player can see 3 move options highlighted on the board at each turn
- FR3: Player can select one of the 3 proposed moves to execute it
- FR4: System generates 3 moves per turn classified by quality tier (Top, Correct, Bof) without revealing the classification to the player
- FR5: AI opponent can play a responding move freely after the player's move
- FR6: System enforces all standard chess rules (legal moves, check, castling, en passant, promotion)
- FR7: Player can resign from a game in progress

### Game Flow

- FR8: Player can start a new game immediately upon app launch (no signup, no barriers)
- FR9: Player can start a new game immediately after a game ends
- FR10: System detects and announces end-of-game conditions (checkmate, stalemate)
- FR11: Player can view game result at end of game (win, loss, draw, resignation)
- FR12: AI opponent responds with an artificial delay to create natural game rhythm

### Game Persistence

- FR13: System automatically saves game state when the player leaves or closes the app
- FR14: System automatically restores a saved game when the player returns
- FR15: Player can view a history of past games

### Settings & Configuration

- FR16: Player can configure the AI opponent's ELO level
- FR17: Player can switch between light and dark visual themes
- FR18: System persists player preferences across sessions

### Offline & PWA

- FR19: Player can play a complete game without network connectivity
- FR20: Player can install the app to their device home screen
- FR21: System caches all required assets for offline functionality after initial load

### Social Sharing

- FR22: System renders rich preview cards (title, description, image) when the app URL is shared on social platforms

### Move Undo

- FR23: Player can cancel their last move within a brief cooldown window (~1.2s) to re-choose from the same 3 options

## Non-Functional Requirements

### Performance

- NFR1: Stockfish move generation completes in <200ms (P95) on mid-range mobile devices (e.g., Samsung Galaxy A-series, iPhone SE)
- NFR2: All UI interactions (tap, transition, overlay) render within <400ms
- NFR3: Initial app load completes in <3 seconds on 4G mobile connection (first visit)
- NFR4: Subsequent app loads (cached via Service Worker) complete in <1 second
- NFR5: Board interactions maintain 60fps — no dropped frames during move animations
- NFR6: Stockfish WASM binary loads asynchronously without blocking first render

### Accessibility

- NFR7: All interactive elements are keyboard-navigable (Tab + Enter)
- NFR8: Move options and board squares have descriptive ARIA labels for screen readers
- NFR9: Color contrast ratios meet WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1) for text, (3:1) for UI components
- NFR10: Animations respect `prefers-reduced-motion` user preference

### Reliability

- NFR11: Game state persists across browser crashes, OS kills, and accidental closures
- NFR12: App functions identically offline and online after initial asset cache
- NFR13: No data loss occurs under any normal usage scenario (close, reload, navigate away)
